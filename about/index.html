About Ahmad Mollaei

01.My Journey

My story with programming started in high school, when a friend showed me something he'd written on a screen. I felt that rare click of this is it. That feeling only deepened through university, where I earned my CS degree from the University of Tabriz. But life, as it sometimes does, had other plans.

I spent years working across freight forwarding, GCC-wide logistics, and automotive diagnostics-industries that seems far from a terminal window, until you look closer. In every role, I was the person automating the tedious parts: building Python scripts to cut costs, integrating AI to speed up decisions, and solving the problem underneath the obvious problem. The industries changed. The instinct never did.

Now I'm building full-time — and with purpose. I focus on developer tools, open-source infrastructure, and AI/ML systems, with a particular drive towards education and diabetes care. As someone living with diabetes myself, I don't see it as just a research area. I see it as a personal responsibility. The right tool, built well, can genuinely change how someone lives. That's the bar I hold myself to.

I work from first principles — I'd rather understand something deeply than move fast on shaky foundations. I have ADHD, which means I sometimes scatter across ideas, but it also means that when something locks me in, nothing else exists. I take small, deliberate steps every day towards a bigger vision, build in public, and contribute to open source as I grow. I'm here to make a name for myself — not through noise, but through craft.
